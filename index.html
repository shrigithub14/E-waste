What is E-Waste?

E-Waste refers to discarded electrical and electronic devices, including computers, televisions, mobile phones, and household appliances. Due to the rapid advancement of technology, the amount of e-waste generated is growing exponentially worldwide.

Why It Matters

Why E-Waste Management is Important

Improper disposal of e-waste can lead to harmful environmental effects and pose health risks to humans and animals.

Reduce Environmental Impact

Proper e-waste disposal prevents toxic substances like lead and mercury from contaminating the soil and water.

Protect Human Health

Many electronic devices contain hazardous materials that, if not handled properly, can affect human health.

Conserve Natural Resources

Recycling e-waste can recover valuable materials like gold, copper, and plastic, reducing the need for new resources.

How E-Waste is Managed

Proper e-waste management involves collection, recycling, and disposal processes designed to minimize environmental and health risks.

Collection

E-waste is collected from households, businesses, and organizations to ensure proper handling.

Recycling

Materials like metals, plastics, and glass are separated and recycled for future use.

Safe Disposal

Hazardous materials are safely disposed of to prevent harm to the environment and public health.
